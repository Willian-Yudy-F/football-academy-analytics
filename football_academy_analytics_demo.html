The Problem

Most football academies are
flying blind on their own data.

How many students didn't renew last term?
▾
Academies typically have no way to see renewal rates by program, location or age group. Each lost student means $300–500 in revenue gone silently.
→ Solution: live dashboard with renewal metrics by segment.
Which parent is about to not renew?
▾
A student misses 3 weeks in a row — nobody notices until they're already gone. Re-engaging a lost student costs 5× more than retaining one.
→ Solution: automated at-risk alert when attendance drops below threshold.
Which student has the highest potential?
▾
Coaches know instinctively but can't prove it to parents or scouts. A parent who sees their child's progress in data renews differently than one who only gets verbal feedback.
→ Solution: automated PDF report per player, emailed at end of term.
What's the actual ROI of marketing channels?
▾
Instagram, Google, referrals — which one actually brings paying students? Without tracking, budget goes to the loudest channel, not the most effective one.
→ Solution: lead source field in enrolment form → live pie chart in dashboard.

Automated Player Report

PDF reports that make
parents renew on the spot.

At the end of each term, every parent receives a personalised PDF with their child's progress — generated and emailed automatically. Zero manual work.

📊Skill radar across 11 technical & tactical attributes
▾
Ball control, passing, dribbling, finishing, positioning, game reading, speed, stamina, attitude, concentration, teamwork — rated by coach via Google Form on phone during each session (3 min).
📈Term-over-term delta with ▲▼ indicators
▾
Each skill shows the variation vs previous term: +0.8 in dribbling, −0.3 in positioning. Parents see progress trends, not just a snapshot. Built with Pandas comparing term averages.
⚡Auto-generated in Python, emailed via smtplib
▾
Script reads Google Sheets via gspread API, calculates averages per player, generates PDF with ReportLab, sends via Gmail SMTP. One command runs everything for the entire academy.
